# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and engineering managers at Israeli and international tech companies. They arrive from a link pasted into a job-application form or a LinkedIn profile, mid-screening, on a laptop in a bright office. They give the page 30 to 90 seconds. Their job: decide whether Tomer is worth an interview. Secondary audience: engineers who will vouch for him after a referral.

## Product Purpose

A single-page personal site for Tomer Kadison's job search. It must make his strongest signals impossible to miss: 4+ years building observability and data platforms in Unit 8200, team leadership, deep Prometheus/VictoriaMetrics/Grafana + Kubernetes ownership, and two shipped side projects (Goodman Chrome extension, Docker Tar). Success = the reviewer opens his CV, GitHub, or email. Deployed as a static site on GitHub Pages.

## Brand Personality

Hands-on, pragmatic, entrepreneurial. An engineer who self-hosts the whole stack and reads the internals. Confident without decoration; playful only in small, earned doses (observability in-jokes like status dots and sparklines). Reads as a person, not a template.

## Anti-references

- The dark-navy "observability = dark mode dashboard" cliché.
- Generic AI-generated developer portfolios: centered hero, icon-title-text card grids, gradient text, glassmorphism.
- Editorial-magazine affectation (italic display serifs, drop caps): wrong voice for a platform engineer.
- Resume-as-wall-of-text with no hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **Skimmable in 30 seconds, rewarding in 3 minutes.** Headline facts up top; depth (role details, project stories) for those who scroll.
2. **Show the discipline.** Observability motifs (time-series lines, status markers, mono metric labels) used as structure, not costume.
3. **Every claim is concrete.** Numbers, technologies, and links over adjectives.
4. **One page, no dead ends.** Every section leads toward contact, CV download, GitHub, or LinkedIn.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast. Honors `prefers-reduced-motion`. Semantic HTML, keyboard-navigable, meaningful link text. English content, LTR.
